This plot applies to the Northern Hemisphere. We can see that while the line of regression indicates almost no correlation between latitude and and cloudiness, the majority of the least cloudy cities in the Northern Hemisphere are around 30-40 degree, while the most cloudy cities are 0-15 or 45-70 degrees. In this case the flat regression line is misleading due to the clustering of cloudines/no cloudiness around certain latitudes.

The sample size for the Southern Hemisphere is smaller than for the Northern, so I would be more skeptical of drawing conclusions. However we do see some clustering of cloudiness/no cloudiness around particular latitudes, indicating the regression line is likely misleading as in the North, and that there is correlation between latitude and cloudiness.
