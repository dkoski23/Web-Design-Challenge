This plot is for the Northern Hemisphere, and as one would expect, there is a strong level of correlation between latitude and maximum temperature. Namely that as one moves towards the poles maximum temperature tends to drop. Addionally the cities with the highest temperatures were at 30-40 degrees latitude, which I found a bit interesting seeing as the world's hottest deserts are 15-30 degrees in the Northern Hemisphere. Perhaps this is due to people living just north of the deserts, but without examining the longitudes this cannot be determined.

In the Southern Hemisphere there seems to be a direct correllation between latitude and max temperature, in that the temperature goes down as one goes further south. The data is quite evenly distributed around the regression line, and we don't see some of the variation that was observed in the Northern Hemisphere.
